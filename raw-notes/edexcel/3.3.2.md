Theme 3.3.2: Costs

1\. Key Definitions

**Fixed Costs (FC):** Do **not vary** with output (e.g., rent,
salaries).

**Variable Costs (VC):** **Vary directly** with output (e.g., raw
materials, hourly wages).

Total Cost (TC): TC = TFC + TVC

Average (Total) Cost (AC): AC = TC / Q

Average Variable Cost (AVC): AVC = TVC / Q

Marginal Cost (MC): MC = ΔTC / ΔQ (The cost of producing one more unit).

2\. Short-Run Cost Curves & The Law of Diminishing Returns

In the **short run**, at least one factor (e.g., capital) is fixed.
Adding more variable factors (e.g., labour) initially increases
productivity but eventually leads to **diminishing marginal returns**.

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 1: Short-Run Cost Curves)\*

Draw axes: **Cost (£)** vs **Quantity (Q)**.

Draw a U-shaped MC curve.

Draw a U-shaped AVC curve. The MC curve cuts through the lowest point of
the AVC curve.

Draw a U-shaped AC curve above the AVC curve. The MC curve cuts through
the lowest point of the AC curve.

The gap between AC and AVC is **Average Fixed Cost (AFC)**, which slopes
downwards.

- Why U-shaped?

  - **Initially:** Increasing returns (specialisation) pull **MC, AVC,
    and AC down**.

  - **Eventually:** Diminishing returns set in, causing **MC to rise**.
    Rising MC pulls up **AVC and AC**.

3\. The Relationship Between MC, AVC, and AC

When **MC \< AVC**, MC pulls **AVC down**.

When **MC \> AVC**, MC pulls **AVC up**.

Therefore, MC cuts AVC at its minimum point. The same logic applies to
the AC curve.

4\. Long-Run Average Cost (LRAC) Curve

In the **long run**, **all factors are variable**. The firm can change
its **scale of production**.

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 2: The LRAC Curve)\*

Draw axes: **Cost (£)** vs **Quantity (Q)**.

Draw several **U-shaped Short-Run AC (SRAC)** curves of different sizes
next to each other.

Draw a **smooth U-shaped curve** that is tangent to (just touches) the
lowest points of each SRAC curve. Label this **LRAC**.

- Why U-shaped?

  - **Economies of Scale:** As output increases, **LRAC
    falls** (increasing returns to scale).

  - **Diseconomies of Scale:** Beyond a certain output, **LRAC
    rises** due to managerial inefficiencies, coordination problems.

5\. The Relationship Between SRAC and LRAC

- The **LRAC** is an **envelope curve** of all possible **SRAC** curves.

- Each **SRAC** represents a firm of a **given fixed capital size**.

- The **LRAC** shows the lowest possible average cost for **any given
  output level** when the firm can choose its optimal scale.

6\. Exam Focus: Key Takeaways

using the law of diminishing returns.

Explain the relationship: MC cuts AVC and AC at their minimum points.

Draw the LRAC curve and explain its shape using economies and
diseconomies of scale.
