Theme 2.5.4: The Impact of Economic Growth

1\. The Controversy of Growth

Economic growth is a primary macroeconomic aim, but it
involves **trade-offs**. Debates now focus on whether to prioritise
growth or broader measures of **societal well-being**.

2\. Benefits of Economic Growth

|  |  |
|----|----|
| Benefit | Explanation |
| ↑ Living Standards | Higher real incomes increase consumption of goods/services. |
| ↓ Absolute Poverty | Growth raises average incomes, lifting people above a basic subsistence level. |
| ↑ Employment | Expanding output creates jobs, reducing unemployment and its social costs. |
| ↑ Government Revenue | Higher incomes and profits increase tax receipts (e.g., income tax, corporation tax, VAT). |
| ↓ Government Spending | Lower unemployment reduces welfare spending (e.g., Jobseeker's Allowance). |
| ↑ Business Investment & Profits | Higher demand and confidence encourage investment, boosting future productive capacity (LRAS). |
| Potential for ↑ Environmental Tech | Higher incomes and profits can fund R&D into cleaner technologies. |

3\. Costs of Economic Growth

|  |  |
|----|----|
| Cost | Explanation |
| Inflationary Pressure | Rapid growth can cause **demand-pull inflation**, eroding purchasing power, especially for those on fixed incomes. |
| Worsened Income Equality | Growth benefits may not be evenly distributed; the **rich often get richer faster**, increasing the gap between rich and poor. |
| Environmental Damage | Increased production/consumption creates **negative externalities** (e.g., pollution, resource depletion, loss of biodiversity). |
| Worsened Work-Life Balance | Growth culture' can lead to longer working hours, stress, and reduced leisure time, harming well-being. |
| Current Account Problems | High domestic demand can suck in **imports**, worsening the trade balance. Inflation can also harm **export competitiveness**. |
| Congestion & Urban Problems | Concentrated growth leads to overcrowding, higher house prices, and strain on infrastructure. |
| ↑ Consumption of Demerit Goods | Higher incomes may be spent on goods with negative externalities (e.g., tobacco, junk food). |

4\. Exam Focus: Key Takeaways

**Analyse** economic growth from multiple perspectives: consumers,
firms, government, environment.

**Evaluate** the trade-offs involved. Growth is not an unambiguous good.

**Discuss** the potential conflict between economic growth and other
macroeconomic objectives (e.g., low inflation, environmental
sustainability, equity).

**Consider** the difference between **quantitative** growth (GDP)
and **qualitative** improvements in living standards and well-being.
