Theme 4.1.2: Specialisation & Trade

1\. Key Concepts

**Absolute Advantage:** A country can produce a good using **fewer
resources** (factors of production) than another country.

**Comparative Advantage (David Ricardo):** A country should specialise
in producing the good where it has the **lowest opportunity cost**. This
is the basis for **mutually beneficial trade**.

2\. Illustrating with PPFs

(Hand-draw Diagram: Two-Country PPFs)

Draw **two PPF diagrams** side-by-side for Country A and Country B, with
two goods (e.g., T-Shirts and Computer Chips).

Label axes and show different maximum outputs to illustrate absolute
advantage.

Calculate Opportunity Cost:

**OC of 1 Computer Chip** = (T-Shirts given up) / (Computer Chips
gained).

**OC of 1 T-Shirt** = (Computer Chips given up) / (T-Shirts gained).

The country with the **lower OC for a good** has the **comparative
advantage** in that good and should specialise in it.

3\. Assumptions of the Comparative Advantage Model

Zero transport costs.

Perfect knowledge.

Factors of production are perfectly mobile within countries.

Constant returns to scale (no economies of scale).

No trade barriers.

4\. Advantages of Specialisation & Trade

Lower prices and greater variety/choice for consumers.

**Efficiency gains** from specialisation and **economies of scale**.

Increased **competition** drives **innovation** and **quality**.

Higher global output, economic growth, and improved living standards.

5\. Disadvantages / Limitations of Specialisation & Trade

**Over-dependence & Vulnerability:** Reliance on imports/volatile global
markets (e.g., energy).

**Structural Unemployment:** Immobile resources cause job losses in
declining industries.

**Environmental Damage:** Increased production/transport leads
to **negative externalities**.

**Inequality:** Benefits unevenly distributed; can widen domestic/global
income gaps.

**Balance of Payments Problems:** Persistent trade deficits for
uncompetitive nations.

Exploitation & Unfair Competition: Dumping (selling below
cost), monopsony power of large firms, challenges for infant
industries in developing countries.

**Loss of Sovereignty/Culture:** Influence of dominant trading partners
and transnational corporations.

6\. Exam Focus: Key Takeaways

**Calculate** opportunity costs and **identify** absolute and
comparative advantage from data/PPFs.

**Draw and use PPF diagrams** to illustrate comparative advantage and
the gains from specialisation.

**State and criticise** the assumptions of comparative advantage theory.

**Analyse** the wide-ranging advantages of international trade.

**Evaluate** the disadvantages, considering impacts on different
stakeholders (e.g., developed vs. developing countries, consumers vs.
workers).
