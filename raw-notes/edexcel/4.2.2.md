Theme 4.2.2: Inequality

1\. Key Definitions

**Income Inequality:** Unequal distribution of **earnings** (wages,
rent, interest, profit) – a **flow**.

**Wealth Inequality:** Unequal distribution of **assets** (property,
shares, savings) – a **stock**.

2\. Measuring Inequality: Lorenz Curve & Gini Coefficient

(Hand-draw Diagram: Lorenz Curve and Gini Coefficient)

Draw a square. Label the bottom axis **"Cumulative % of
Households"** (0-100%). Label the left axis **"Cumulative % of
Income"** (0-100%).

Draw a **45° line** from the origin. Label it **"Line of Perfect
Equality"**.

Draw a **curved line (Lorenz Curve)** below the 45° line. The more it
bows out, the greater the inequality.

Shade the area between the 45° line and the Lorenz Curve. Label this
area **A**.

Shade the area under the Lorenz Curve. Label this area **B**.

- Gini Coefficient = A / (A + B)

  - **0 = Perfect equality** (Lorenz Curve is the 45° line).

  - **1 = Perfect inequality** (one person has all income).

  - A higher Gini indicates greater inequality.

3\. Causes of Income & Wealth Inequality

|  |  |
|----|----|
| Factor | Effect on Inequality |
| Differences in Education/Skills | Higher skills → higher wages → greater inequality. |
| Strength of Trade Unions | Strong unions raise wages for workers, reducing inequality. |
| Wage Rates & Employment Laws | **National Minimum Wage (NMW)** reduces inequality. Weak labour laws increase it. |
| Tax and Benefit System | Progressive taxes and generous welfare benefits reduce inequality. |
| Asset Ownership & Wealth | Wealth generates more income (e.g., rent, dividends), widening inequality over time. |

4\. The Impact of Economic Development: The Kuznets Curve

(Hand-draw Diagram: The Kuznets Curve)

Draw axes: X-axis = Economic Development (GDP per capita), Y-axis =
Income Inequality.

Draw an inverted U-shaped curve.

**Hypothesis:** As an economy develops (moves from agriculture to
industry), inequality **first increases**, then **decreases** after a
turning point due to broader wealth distribution and government
intervention.

5\. Capitalism and Inequality

- Capitalism inherently generates inequality due to differences in
  skills, entrepreneurship, and asset ownership.

- The **profit motive and incentives** drive efficiency and growth but
  can lead to **concentrated wealth**.

- This creates a role for **government intervention** (e.g., progressive
  taxation, welfare, education) to mitigate inequality while preserving
  incentives.
