Theme 3.1.2: Business Growth

1\. Organic (Internal) Growth

**Definition:** Growth generated **from within the business** using its
own resources.

**Methods:** Increasing market share, new products (diversification),
new locations, investment in technology, international expansion.

**Pace:** Generally **slower** and more controlled.

2\. Inorganic (External) Growth

Growth through **mergers** (joining) or **takeovers** (buying) other
firms.

*(Hand-draw Diagram: Vertical Integration)*\
Draw a simple supply chain: **RAW MATERIALS --\> MANUFACTURER --\>
RETAILER**. Draw an arrow from Manufacturer pointing **left** to Raw
Materials labelled **"Backward Integration"**. Draw another arrow from
Manufacturer pointing **right** to Retailer labelled **"Forward
Integration"**.

- **Horizontal Integration:** Merger with a **competitor** at the **same
  stage** of production (e.g., two car manufacturers merge).
  Increases **market share**.

- Vertical Integration:

  - **Backward:** With a supplier (**earlier** in supply chain). Secures
    inputs, controls quality/cost.

  - **Forward:** With a customer (**later** in supply chain). Secures
    outlets, captures profit margin.

- **Conglomerate Integration:** Merger with a firm in a **completely
  different industry** (e.g., a car firm buys a food
  chain). **Diversifies risk**.

3\. Advantages & Disadvantages

|  |  |  |
|----|----|----|
| Type of Growth | Key Advantages | Key Disadvantages |
| Organic | Lower risk, financed by profit, retains control/ culture. | Slow, may miss economies of scale, limited finance. |
| Horizontal Integration | Rapid market share gain, **economies of scale**, reduces competition. | **Diseconomies of scale**, culture clash, may attract regulator attention. |
| Vertical Integration | Control supply chain, secure inputs/outlets, reduce costs, improve quality. | **Diseconomies of scale**, lack of expertise in new stage, high cost. |
| Conglomerate | Diversifies risk (spreads it), potential for high returns from new markets. | Lack of expertise, complex to manage, **diseconomies of scale**. |

4\. Constraints on Business Growth

**Size of the Market:** Niche or saturated markets limit expansion (may
require internationalisation).

**Access to Finance:** Smaller firms face higher perceived risk, leading
to difficulty/ higher cost of borrowing.

**Owner Objectives:** Owners may
prioritise **lifestyle/satificing** over maximising growth
(principal-agent problem).

**Government Regulation:** **Competition policy** (e.g., CMA) can block
mergers that create excessive market power. Regulations on demerit goods
can also limit growth.

5\. Exam Focus: Key Takeaways

**Define and distinguish** between organic and inorganic growth.

**Identify and explain** the three types of integration (horizontal,
vertical, conglomerate) with examples.

**Draw** a diagram to illustrate forward/backward vertical integration.

**Analyse** the potential advantages and disadvantages of different
growth strategies.

**Evaluate** the factors that can constrain business growth.
