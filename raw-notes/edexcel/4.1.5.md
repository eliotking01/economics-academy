Theme 4.1.5: Trading Blocs and the WTO

1\. Types of Trading Blocs (Increasing Integration)

**Free Trade Area (FTA):** Members remove trade barriers **between
themselves** but keep independent trade policies with non-members (e.g.,
USMCA).

**Customs Union:** An FTA **plus** a **common external tariff** on
imports from non-members (e.g., the EU's customs union).

Common Market: A customs union plus free movement of factors of
production (labour, capital).

**Monetary Union:** A common market **plus** a **single currency** and
common monetary policy (e.g., Eurozone).

2\. Costs and Benefits of Regional Trade Agreements

Benefits (Trade Creation):

**Increased Efficiency:** Specialisation according to comparative
advantage lowers costs.

Lower Prices & Greater Choice: For consumers within the bloc.

**Economies of Scale:** Larger market allows firms to grow.

**(For Monetary Unions):** Reduced transaction costs & exchange rate
uncertainty.

Costs:

**Trade Diversion:** Trade shifts from a more
efficient **non-member** to a less efficient **member** due to tariff
advantages, reducing global efficiency.

**Loss of Sovereignty:** Especially in monetary/customs unions (e.g.,
cannot set independent trade or interest rate policy).

**Structural Unemployment:** Inefficient domestic industries may
decline.

**Bloc can become Fortress-like:** High common external tariffs reduce
trade with the rest of the world.

3\. Conditions for a Successful Monetary Union

**Synchronised Business Cycles:** So one interest rate suits all
members.

**Labour & Capital Mobility:** To adjust to asymmetric shocks (e.g.,
workers moving from high to low unemployment regions).

**Fiscal Transfers:** A central budget to support struggling regions
(an **automatic stabiliser**).

Similar Inflation Rates & Competitiveness: To prevent persistent
imbalances.

4\. The Role of the World Trade Organisation (WTO)

**Purpose:** To **promote trade liberalisation** (reducing barriers) and
oversee global trade rules.

Key Functions:

**Negotiating Forum:** Hosts rounds of talks (e.g., Doha Round) to
reduce tariffs and quotas globally.

**Dispute Settlement:** Acts as a court to resolve trade disputes
between member countries.

**Conflict with Trading Blocs:** While the WTO promotes **global** free
trade, **regional blocs** are inherently **discriminatory** (favouring
members). This can lead to **trade diversion** and conflict with the
WTO's non-discrimination principle.

Key Exam Tips:

Crucial Distinction: Know the exact differences between Free Trade Area,
Customs Union, and Monetary Union. The common external tariff is the key
feature of a customs union.

**Trade Creation vs. Diversion:** You **must** be able to explain,
identify, and evaluate these two effects. Trade creation is a gain in
efficiency; trade diversion is a loss.

**Evaluation of Monetary Unions:** The Eurozone is a prime case study.
Evaluate its success/failure using the conditions above (e.g., lack of
full fiscal integration and labour mobility are often cited weaknesses).

**WTO vs. Blocs:** Recognise the tension. Trading blocs can be
a **stepping stone** or a **stumbling block** to full global free trade.
