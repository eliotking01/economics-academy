Theme 3.4.3: Monopolistic Competition

1\. Key Characteristics

Many small firms.

**Differentiated products** (via branding, quality, location, service).
This is the **key feature**.

Low barriers to entry and exit.

**Some price-setting power** (they are **price makers** due to product
differentiation).

2\. The Firm's Demand Curve

Because products are differentiated, each firm faces a downward-sloping
demand (AR) curve. They have a small amount of market power.

3\. Short-Run Equilibrium: Profit & Loss

The firm maximises profit where **MC = MR**.

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 1: Short-Run Supernormal Profit)\*

Draw the firm's **MC** and **AC** curves.

Draw a downward-sloping AR (Demand) curve and a steeper MR curve below
it.

Find **Q1** where **MC = MR**.

At Q1, find price **P1** on the AR curve and cost **C1** on the AC
curve.

If P1 \> C1, the firm makes supernormal profit. Shade the area.

Firms can also make **losses** in the short run if AR \< AC at the
profit-maximising output.

4\. Long-Run Equilibrium: Normal Profit Only

Due to **low barriers to entry/exit**, the market adjusts to long-run
equilibrium where all firms make **normal profit**.

**Process:** Short-run **supernormal profit** attracts **new entrants**.
This **reduces the market share** (shifts the demand curve **left**) for
each existing firm until supernormal profit is eliminated. The reverse
happens with losses (firms exit, demand shifts right).

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 2: Long-Run Equilibrium)\*

Draw the firm's **MC** and **AC** curves.

Draw a **downward-sloping AR** curve such that it is **tangent to the AC
curve**. Draw the corresponding **MR** curve.

Find **Qlr** where **MC = MR**.

At Qlr, the price **Plr** on the AR curve equals the average
cost **Clr** on the AC curve. **AR = AC**. The firm earns **normal
profit**.

5\. Efficiency in Long-Run Equilibrium

**Productive Efficiency: NO.** The firm does **not** produce at the
minimum point of AC (not where MC=AC). There is **excess capacity** (Qlr
is left of the min AC point).

**Allocative Efficiency: NO.** At Qlr, **P \> MC**. The firm
is **under-producing** relative to the social optimum.

**Dynamic Efficiency:** **Possible, but limited.** Some supernormal
profit may be earned in the short run for innovation, but it is competed
away in the long run.

6\. Exam Focus: Key Takeaways

**List** the characteristics, highlighting **product
differentiation** and **low barriers**.

**Draw** the **long-run equilibrium diagram** for a monopolistically
competitive firm, showing the AR curve tangent to the AC curve.

**Explain** the **adjustment process** from short-run profit/loss to
long-run normal profit.

Analyse the inefficiencies: the firm is neither productively nor
allocatively efficient in the long run (excess capacity, P \> MC).

**Compare** with perfect competition: both make normal profit in the
long run, but monopolistic competition has differentiated products and
is inefficient.
