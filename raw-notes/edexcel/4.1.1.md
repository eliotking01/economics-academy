Theme 4.1.1: Globalisation

1\. What is Globalisation?

Globalisation is the **process of increasing integration and
interdependence** between the world's economies. It is characterised by:

Increased **free trade** in goods and services.

Greater cross-border capital flows (finance/investment).

Increased **foreign ownership** of firms (growth of MNCs/TNCs).

Greater international mobility of labour and technology.

2\. Factors Contributing to Globalisation

- **Trade Liberalisation:** Reduction of trade barriers (tariffs,
  quotas) via the **WTO** and trade blocs.

- **Technology:** Containerisation, the internet, and improved transport
  have drastically reduced the **costs of trade and communication**.

- **Financial Deregulation:** Easier movement of capital across borders.

- **Political Changes:** The end of the Cold War opened up new markets.

- **Growth of Multinational Corporations (MNCs):** Firms that produce
  and operate in multiple countries drive integration.

3\. Impacts of Globalisation

On Consumers:

- **Benefits:** Lower prices, greater variety, improved quality from
  global competition.

- **Costs:** Potential loss of local culture, product homogenisation.

On Workers:

- **Benefits:** New job opportunities, especially in developing
  economies; potential for higher wages.

- **Costs:** **Structural unemployment** in developed nations (e.g.,
  from deindustrialisation), job insecurity, potential exploitation in
  low-cost countries.

On Firms (MNCs):

- **Benefits:** Access to larger markets, lower production costs (global
  supply chains), economies of scale.

- **Costs:** Increased global competition, complex management, political
  risks.

On Governments/Economies:

- **Benefits:** Higher GDP from trade and investment, transfer of
  technology and skills.

- **Costs:** Reduced policy sovereignty (e.g., tax competition),
  increased inequality, vulnerability to global shocks.

On the Environment:

- **Costs:** Increased carbon emissions from transport,
  over-exploitation of resources, "race to the bottom" in environmental
  standards.

Key Exam Tips:

- **Stakeholder Analysis:** Always evaluate impacts from multiple
  perspectives (consumers, workers, firms, governments, environment).
  Impacts are **not uniform**.

- **Link to Concepts:** Connect globalisation to **specialisation,
  comparative advantage, FDI, and inequality**. It is a major driver of
  both growth and structural change.

- **Evaluation:** Globalisation creates **winners and losers**. The
  overall net effect is debated. Criticisms include rising
  inequality, **'race to the bottom'** on regulation, and power of MNCs.

- **Recent Context:** Be aware of trends like **slowbalisation**, rising
  protectionism, and reshoring, which suggest the hyper-globalisation
  phase may be changing.
