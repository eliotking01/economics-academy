Theme 4.1.8: Exchange Rates

1\. Exchange Rate Systems

**Floating Exchange Rate:** Value determined by **market forces** of
demand and supply for the currency on the forex market.

**Appreciation:** Increase in currency value. **Depreciation:** Decrease
in value.

**Fixed Exchange Rate:** Value pegged to another currency (e.g., US
dollar) by the Central Bank.

**Revaluation:** Official raising of the peg. **Devaluation:** Official
lowering of the peg.

**Managed Exchange Rate (Dirty Float):** Central Bank allows market
forces but **intervenes** to stabilise the currency within a target
band.

Changes in **demand** or **supply** for a currency cause its value to
fluctuate.

|  |  |
|----|----|
| Factor causing an **APPRECIATION** | Factor causing a **DEPRECIATION** |
| ↑ Relative Interest Rates (hot money inflows) | ↓ Relative Interest Rates (hot money outflows) |
| ↓ **Relative Inflation Rate** (exports more competitive) | ↑ **Relative Inflation Rate** (exports less competitive) |
| ↑ Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) into UK | ↑ FDI by UK firms abroad |
| Current Account Surplus ↑ | Current Account Deficit ↑ |
| **Positive Speculation** about currency/UK economy | Quantitative Easing (QE) (↑ money supply) |

3\. Government Intervention (Managed Systems)

- To **Appreciate:** **Buy own currency** (using foreign reserves)
  or **Raise interest rates**.

- To **Depreciate:** **Sell own currency** (adding to reserves)
  or **Lower interest rates**.

4\. Impacts of Exchange Rate Changes

a\) Impacts of a DEPRECIATION (e.g., £ falls in value)

- **Exports:** **Cheaper** for foreigners → Quantity demanded ↑ (if
  PED\>1). **Imports:** **Dearer** for UK consumers → Quantity demanded
  ↓.

- **Current Account (CA):** Likely to **improve** over time if
  the **Marshall-Lerner condition** holds (PEDx + PEDm \>1). Short-run
  deterioration possible (**J-Curve effect**).

- **Economic Growth:** ↑ Net Exports (X-M) → ↑ AD → growth.

- **Inflation:** **Cost-push** (imported raw materials dearer) &
  potential **demand-pull**.

- **Unemployment:** Likely ↓ if export industries grow.

- **Living Standards:** Mixed. Imported goods cost more, but jobs/wages
  may rise.

b\) Impacts of an APPRECIATION (Opposite effects)

5\. Competitive Devaluation

- **Definition:** When a government **deliberately depreciates** its
  currency to make its exports cheaper and gain a trade advantage.

- **Risks:** **Retaliation** (trade/currency wars), rising **import
  costs**, can be **inflationary**.
