Theme 4.4.1: Role of Financial Markets

1\. Main Functions of Financial Markets

Financial markets (e.g., stock markets, bond markets, banking systems)
are crucial for economic activity. Their key roles are:

**To Facilitate Saving:** Provide a secure way for households/firms to
store money, which then forms a pool of funds available for lending.

To Lend to Businesses and Individuals: Provide credit (loans, mortgages)
which finances consumption and investment, accelerating economic growth.

**To Facilitate the Exchange of Goods/Services:** Provide the payment
systems (cards, bank transfers, digital apps) that make everyday
transactions possible.

**To Provide a Market for Equities (Shares):** Allow companies to raise
long-term finance by selling shares (**primary market**) and allow
investors to trade these shares (**secondary market**).

**To Provide a Market for Debt (Bonds):** Allow governments and firms to
borrow by issuing bonds, raising capital for public spending and
investment.

**To Facilitate Forward Markets (Futures):** Allow trading
in **commodities and currencies** at a price agreed today for future
delivery. This helps firms **hedge** against price risks.

2\. Broader Economic Importance

**Capital Allocation:** They channel savings towards the most productive
investments, improving **allocative efficiency**.

**Price Determination:** They determine **market prices** for financial
assets (share prices, interest rates, exchange rates), which signal
information to the whole economy.

**Risk Management:** They provide instruments (e.g., insurance, futures
contracts) that allow firms to manage financial risks.

Key Exam Tips:

**Link to Growth:** The ability to channel savings into
productive **investment** is the core link between functioning financial
markets and **long-run economic growth**.

**Market Failure:** Financial markets can fail (e.g., **asymmetric
information** leading to crises, **speculative bubbles**). This often
justifies **regulation** (e.g., by the Financial Conduct Authority).

**Real-World Examples:** Use the **2008 Financial Crisis** to illustrate
the catastrophic consequences when financial markets fail (e.g., through
excessive risk-taking and poor lending).

**Evaluation:** While essential, highly complex and deregulated
financial markets can increase **systemic risk** and instability for the
real economy.
