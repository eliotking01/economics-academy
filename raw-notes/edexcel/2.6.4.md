Theme 2.6.4: Conflicts & Trade-Offs Between Objectives & Policies

1\. The Core Problem

Governments face **trade-offs** when trying to achieve all macroeconomic
objectives simultaneously. Improving one objective often worsens
another.

2\. Key Trade-Offs Between Objectives

|  |  |
|----|----|
| Trade-Off | Explanation |
| Economic Growth vs. Low Inflation | Rapid growth can cause **demand-pull inflation** as the economy overheats. |
| Economic Growth vs. Environmental Sustainability | Growth often increases pollution, resource depletion, and negative externalities. |
| Economic Growth vs. Income Equality | Growth benefits capital owners (profits) disproportionately, potentially **widening inequality**. |
| Economic Growth vs. Current Account Balance | Growth boosts incomes, increasing **demand for imports**, worsening the trade balance. |
| Low Unemployment vs. Low Inflation | The **Phillips Curve** suggests a short-run trade-off: lower unemployment risks higher wage inflation. |

3\. The Short-Run Phillips Curve (SRPC)

(Hand-draw Diagram: The Short-Run Phillips Curve)

Draw axes: Y-axis = Inflation Rate, X-axis = Unemployment Rate.

Draw a downward-sloping curve labelled SRPC.

Mark point A on the curve (low unemployment, high inflation). Mark point
B (high unemployment, low inflation).

**What it shows:** A **short-run inverse relationship** between
unemployment and inflation.

**Reason:** As AD rises, output and employment increase (↓unemployment),
but this bids up wages and prices (↑inflation). The reverse is also
true.

**Limitation:** This is a **short-run** trade-off. In the long run, the
Phillips Curve is **vertical** at the **Natural Rate of Unemployment
(NRU)**.

4\. Policy Conflicts & Trade-Offs

Policy tools themselves can create conflicts between short-term and
long-term goals.

|  |  |
|----|----|
| Policy Conflict | Explanation |
| Monetary Policy: Inflation vs. Growth | Raising interest rates to **control inflation** (contractionary) also **discourages investment**, harming long-run supply-side growth. |
| Fiscal Policy: Short-run vs. Long-run | **Increased government spending** on infrastructure boosts AD (short-run demand) and **also increases LRAS** (long-run supply). However, it can cause **short-run inflation** and **budget deficits**. |
| Supply-Side Policy: Efficiency vs. Equity | **Market-based policies** (e.g., reducing min. wage, weakening unions) may increase efficiency and LRAS but can **worsen income inequality**. |
| Environmental Policy vs. Growth | Strict environmental regulations can **increase costs for firms**, potentially reducing short-run output and LRAS in polluting industries, conflicting with growth objectives. |

5\. Exam Focus: Key Takeaways

**Identify and explain** the main trade-offs between macroeconomic
objectives.

**Draw and interpret** the Short-Run Phillips Curve, explaining the
relationship it depicts.

**Analyse** specific policy conflicts (e.g., using high interest rates
to cut inflation but harming investment).

**Evaluate** the difficulty of achieving all objectives at once,
understanding that policy choices inevitably involve **prioritisation**.
