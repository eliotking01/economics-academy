Theme 2.6.2: Demand-Side Policies

1\. What are Demand-Side Policies?

Policies used by the government or central bank to **shift the Aggregate
Demand (AD) curve** to achieve macroeconomic objectives (low inflation,
low unemployment, growth).

2\. Types of Demand-Side Policy

Monetary Policy: Managed by the Bank of England (independent).
Uses interest rates and quantitative easing (QE).

Fiscal Policy: Managed by the Government. Uses government spending
(G) and taxation (T).

3\. Monetary Policy in Detail

Expansionary: Lower interest rates / Increase QE → Stimulates AD.

**Contractionary:** **Raise interest rates** / **Reduce QE** → Slows AD
to control inflation.

Transmission Mechanisms for Interest Rate Changes:

**Cost of Borrowing:** Lower rates make loans/mortgages cheaper → ↑
Consumption (C) & Investment (I).

**Saving Incentive:** Lower rates discourage saving → ↑ C.

**Asset Prices:** Lower rates boost house/share prices (wealth effect) →
↑ C.

**Exchange Rate:** Lower rates can cause currency depreciation → ↑
Exports (X), ↓ Imports (M) → ↑ AD.

**Quantitative Easing (QE):** Central bank creates money to buy bonds →
increases bank liquidity → lowers long-term interest rates → encourages
lending → ↑ C & I.

4\. Fiscal Policy in Detail

Expansionary: Increase G and/or Decrease T → Boosts AD.

Contractionary: Decrease G and/or Increase T → Reduces AD.

Types of Taxation:

**Direct Taxes:** On **income/profits** (e.g., Income Tax, Corporation
Tax). Affect incentives.

**Indirect Taxes:** On **spending** (e.g., VAT). Affect costs and
prices.

5\. The Government Budget

**Budget Deficit:** **G \> T** (requires borrowing, adds to national
debt).

**Budget Surplus:** **T \> G** (can be used to repay debt).

Balanced Budget: G = T.

8\. Exam Focus: Key Takeaways

**Distinguish** between monetary and fiscal policy and their main
instruments.

**Explain** the transmission mechanism of an interest rate change.

**Draw** AD/AS diagrams to show the impact of
expansionary/contractionary policies.

**Analyse** the likely effects of a specific policy change on key
objectives (growth, inflation, unemployment).

**Evaluate** the effectiveness of demand-side policies using
strengths/weaknesses and in different contexts (e.g., deep recession vs.
boom).
