Theme 2.5.1: Causes of Economic Growth

1\. Actual vs. Potential Growth

**Actual Growth:** An increase in **real GDP** over time. Measured by
the percentage change in the **actual output** of goods/services.

**Potential Growth:** An increase in the economy's **productive
capacity**. This is the **maximum possible output** the economy could
produce if all resources were fully employed.

2\. Short-Run Economic Growth (Demand-Side Growth)

Cause: An increase in Aggregate Demand (AD).

Triggered by: An increase in any component of AD: Consumption (C),
Investment (I), Government Spending (G), or Net Exports (X-M).

**Result:** The economy moves **closer to** its existing productive
potential (moves from inside the PPF towards the frontier).

**Diagrammatic Representation**\
\*(Hand-draw Diagram 1: Short-Run Growth on AD/AS)\*

Draw AD/AS axes. Draw an upward-sloping SRAS curve.

Draw AD1 intersecting SRAS at output **Y1**.

Shift AD1 **rightwards** to AD2.

Show the new equilibrium at a **higher Real GDP (Y2)** and a higher
Price Level. This is **actual growth**.

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 2: Short-Run Growth on a PPF)\*

Draw a PPF curve.

Mark a point **inside** the curve (e.g., point X) representing
under-utilisation.

Mark a new point **closer to** the curve (e.g., point Y).

The movement **X** → **Y** represents short-run growth through better
use of existing resources.

3\. Long-Run Economic Growth (Supply-Side Growth)

**Cause:** An increase in the **quality or quantity** of the factors of
production (land, labour, capital, enterprise).

Result: An outward shift of the Long-Run Aggregate Supply (LRAS) curve
and the Production Possibility Frontier (PPF). This is an increase
in potential output.

**Diagrammatic Representation**\
\*(Hand-draw Diagram 3: Long-Run Growth on AD/AS)\*

Draw AD/AS axes. Draw a vertical **LRAS1** curve at potential
output **Yfe1**.

Draw AD and SRAS intersecting at Yfe1.

Shift **LRAS1 rightwards** to **LRAS2** at new potential
output **Yfe2**.

Explain that this allows for a **new long-run equilibrium** with higher
sustainable output.

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 4: Long-Run Growth on a PPF)\*

Draw an initial PPF curve.

Draw a new PPF curve **outside** the original.

This **outward shift** represents an increase in the economy's
productive potential.

4\. Factors Causing Long-Run (Supply-Side) Growth

These factors shift the **LRAS curve outwards**:

|  |  |
|----|----|
| Factor | How it increases potential output |
| Technological Advances | ↑ Quality of capital → More efficient production processes. |
| Investment in Capital | ↑ Quantity/quality of physical capital (machinery, infrastructure). |
| Improvements in Human Capital | ↑ Quality of labour via better education, training, and healthcare. |
| Demographic Changes | ↑ Quantity of labour via higher population growth or positive net migration. |
| Increased Competition & Enterprise | More efficient markets and innovation driven by competition policy. |
| Institutional Improvements | Better legal systems, property rights, and political stability encourage investment. |

5\. Export-Led Growth

**Concept:** A strategy where economic growth is primarily driven by
a **sustained increase in exports (X)**.

**Mechanism:** Rising exports directly increase **AD** (short-run
growth). The revenue and investment generated can then fund improvements
in **productive capacity** (long-run growth).

**Example:** China's rapid growth from the 1990s-2010s was heavily
driven by becoming the 'world's factory'.

**Risks:** Can create over-dependence on global demand, making the
economy vulnerable to external shocks.

6\. Exam Focus: Key Takeaways

**Distinguish** clearly between **short-run** (demand-side, AD increase)
and **long-run** (supply-side, LRAS shift) growth.

**Draw** both **AD/AS** and **PPF diagrams** to illustrate both types of
growth.

**List and explain** the factors that cause long-run supply-side growth.

**Analyse** the concept of **export-led growth** and evaluate its
benefits and risks.

**Understand** that sustainable long-term growth requires an expansion
of **productive potential** (LRAS), not just temporary increases in AD.
