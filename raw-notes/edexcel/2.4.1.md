Theme 2.4.1: National Income

1\. The Circular Flow of Income

This model illustrates how money moves between different sectors
(agents) in an economy.

The Basic Model (Closed Economy, No Government)

Two Agents: Households and Firms.

**Real Flows:** Households supply **factors of production** (land,
labour, capital, enterprise) to firms. Firms supply **goods and
services** to households.

**Money Flows:** Firms pay **income** (rent, wages, interest, profit) to
households. Households spend this as **consumer expenditure** on
goods/services, which is revenue for firms.

2\. Injections (J) and Withdrawals/Leakages (W)

The basic model is expanded to include the **Government**, **Financial
Sector**, and **Foreign Trade**. This introduces flows that change the
size of the circular flow.

**Injections (J):** Additions of **new money** into the circular flow.

**Investment (I):** Spending by firms on capital goods.

**Government Spending (G):** Spending by the state on public
goods/services.

**Exports (X):** Revenue from overseas consumers buying domestic goods.

Withdrawals/Leakages (W): Money leaving the circular flow.

**Savings (S):** Household income not spent.

**Taxation (T):** Money paid to the government.

**Imports (M):** Money spent on goods from abroad.

3\. Equilibrium in the Circular Flow

- National Income is in equilibrium when Total Injections = Total
  Withdrawals.

  - J = W or I + G + X = S + T + M

- If Injections \> Withdrawals, the circular flow expands → Economic
  Growth.

- If **Withdrawals \> Injections**, the circular flow **contracts** →
  Economic Decline/Recession.

4\. National Income: Key Concepts

**National Income:** The **total value of output, expenditure, and
income** generated in an economy over a period of time (usually a year).

The Three Measures (which should be equal):

Output Method: Value of all goods and services produced.

**Income Method:** Sum of all **incomes** earned (wages, rent, interest,
profit).

Expenditure Method: Sum of all spending (AD = C + I + G + (X-M)).

**Income vs. Wealth:** **Income** is a **flow** concept (measured over
time, e.g., £ per year). **Wealth** is a **stock** concept (the total
value of assets owned at a point in time).

5\. Exam Focus: Key Takeaways

**Draw and explain** the basic circular flow of income between
households and firms.

Define and identify the three injections (I, G, X) and three
withdrawals (S, T, M).

**Explain** the condition for equilibrium in the circular flow (**J =
W**) and analyse the consequences of inequalities (J \> W or J \< W).

**Understand** that national income can be measured in three ways
(output, income, expenditure) which are equivalent.

**Distinguish** between the **flow** of income and the **stock** of
wealth.
