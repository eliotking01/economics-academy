Theme 3.4.1: Efficiency

1\. Types of Efficiency

|  |  |  |
|----|----|----|
| Type of Efficiency | Condition (Where it occurs) | Meaning & Implication |
| Allocative Efficiency | Price (P) = Marginal Cost (MC) | Resources are allocated to produce the **optimal mix of goods and services** that society wants. Maximum social welfare. |
| Productive Efficiency | Produce at the lowest point on the Average Cost (AC) curve (where MC = AC). | Production at **minimum cost**. No waste of resources. |
| Dynamic Efficiency | Over time, through investment in R&D, innovation, and new technology. | Improves product quality and lowers costs in the long run. Linked to supernormal profits for reinvestment. |
| X-Inefficiency | Firms operate **above** their AC curve. | **Lack of competition** leads to complacency, waste, and higher costs than necessary. |

2\. Efficiency in Perfect Competition

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 1: Perfect Competition & Efficiency)\*

Draw axes for a **single firm** in a perfectly competitive market.

Draw the horizontal **P = AR = MR** line (market price).

Draw the firm's **MC** and **AC** curves.

Show the firm's profit-maximising output **Qpc** where **P = MC**.

At Qpc, also show that **P = MC = min AC**.

**Conclusion:** In **long-run equilibrium**, a perfectly competitive
market is **both allocatively (P=MC) and productively (min AC)
efficient**. However, it may lack **dynamic efficiency** due to the
absence of supernormal profits for reinvestment.

3\. Efficiency in Imperfect Competition (Monopoly/Price Maker)

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 2: Imperfect Competition & Inefficiency)\*

Draw axes for a firm with market power.

Draw downward-sloping AR and MR curves.

Draw **MC** and **AC** curves.

Find the profit-maximising output **Qm** where **MC = MR**.

From Qm, find the price **Pm** on the AR curve and the average
cost **Cm** on the AC curve.

- Inefficiency Identified:

  - **Allocative Inefficiency:** At Qm, **Pm \> MC**. Society values an
    extra unit more than it costs to produce it → **under-production**.

  - **Productive Inefficiency:** At Qm, the firm is **not** producing at
    the lowest point on the AC curve (not where MC=AC) → **excess
    costs**.

- **Potential for Dynamic Efficiency:** The **supernormal profit** (Pm
  \> Cm) can be **reinvested in R&D**, potentially leading to innovation
  and dynamic efficiency.

4\. Summary of Efficiency by Market Structure

|  |  |  |  |  |
|----|----|----|----|----|
| Market Structure | Allocative Efficiency? | Productive Efficiency? | Dynamic Efficiency? | Risk of X-Inefficiency? |
| Perfect Competition | **Yes** (LR: P=MC) | **Yes** (LR: min AC) | **Unlikely** (no supernormal profit) | **Low** (competition forces cost-minimisation) |
| Monopoly | **No** (P \> MC) | **No** (not at min AC) | **Possible** (supernormal profit funds R&D) | **High** (lack of competition) |
