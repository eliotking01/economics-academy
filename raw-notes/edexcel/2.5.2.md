Theme 2.5.2: Output Gaps

1\. Key Concept: The Trend Rate of Growth

This is the **long-run average increase** in an economy's productive
potential (LRAS). It is the sustainable growth rate without causing
inflation.

**Actual GDP** fluctuates around this trend, creating **output gaps**.

2\. What is an Output Gap?

**Definition:** The difference between the **actual level of real
GDP** and the **potential level of real GDP** (the output level when the
economy is at full capacity, Yfe).

Formula: Output Gap = Actual GDP – Potential GDP

3\. Negative Output Gap (Recessionary Gap)

**Occurs when:** **Actual GDP \< Potential GDP** (the economy is
producing *below* its capacity).

**Characteristics:** Spare capacity, **unemployment** of resources
(especially cyclical unemployment), low inflation/deflationary pressure.

Cause: Typically due to a deficiency of Aggregate Demand (AD).

4\. Positive Output Gap (Inflationary Gap)

**Occurs when:** **Actual GDP \> Potential GDP** (the economy is
producing *above* its sustainable capacity).

**Characteristics:** Resources are used **unsustainably** (e.g., extreme
overtime, overuse of machinery), leading to **demand-pull inflation**,
labour shortages, and a current account deficit.

Cause: Typically due to excessive Aggregate Demand (AD).

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 2: Positive Output Gap)\*

Draw AD/AS axes with a vertical **LRAS** at **Yfe**.

Draw an **AD curve** intersecting a upward-sloping **SRAS curve** to
the **right** of LRAS.

Mark the equilibrium output **Y1**.

Clearly shade/highlight the horizontal distance between **Yfe and Y1**.
Label this **"Positive Output Gap"**.

This represents an economic boom.

5\. The Role of Economic Views

**Classical View:** Output gaps are **temporary**. The
economy **self-corrects** via price/wage flexibility back to Yfe. A
negative gap leads to falling prices/wages, stimulating AD. A positive
gap leads to rising costs, reducing AS.

**Keynesian View:** The economy can be **stuck in a negative output
gap** (e.g., a depression) due to price/wage stickiness and low
confidence. **Government intervention** (fiscal policy) is required to
boost AD and close the gap.

6\. Difficulties in Measuring Output Gaps

- Potential GDP (Yfe) is not directly observable; it must be estimated,
  which is imprecise.

- **Indicators** are used to infer the gap:

  - **Negative Gap:** High unemployment, low inflation, low capacity
    utilisation.

  - **Positive Gap:** Low unemployment, rising inflation, reports of
    skill shortages.
