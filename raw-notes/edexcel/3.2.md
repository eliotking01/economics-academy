Theme 3.2: Business Objectives

1\. Profit Maximisation

**Assumed objective** in traditional theory. Follows the **MC = MR
rule**.

**Why?** Maximises returns for **shareholders** (dividends, share
price).

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 1: Profit Maximisation)\*

Draw axes: Cost/Revenue (£) vs Quantity (Q).

Draw a U-shaped MC curve and a U-shaped AC curve.

Draw a downward-sloping AR (Demand) curve and a steeper downward-sloping
MR curve below it.

Mark the point where **MC = MR**. Drop a line down to the quantity axis:
label this **Qpm**.

From Qpm, go up to the AR curve: label this price **Ppm**.

From Qpm, go up to the AC curve: label this average cost **C**.

Shade the area of **supernormal profit**: **(Ppm - C) × Qpm**.

2\. Revenue Maximisation

Objective: Maximise total sales revenue (TR).

**Why?** To gain **market share**, benefit from **economies of scale**,
or due to **principal-agent problem** (e.g., sales managers on
commission).

**Rule:** Produce where **MR = 0**.

*(Adapt Diagram 1 for Revenue Max)*\
On the same diagram, find the quantity where **MR = 0**. Label
this **Qrm**. Go up to the AR curve: label this lower price **Prm**.
Show that **Qrm \> Qpm** and **Prm \< Ppm**. Profit at Qrm is lower.

3\. Sales (Volume) Maximisation

Objective: Maximise the quantity of units sold.

**Why?** To clear stock, dominate the market, or meet sales targets.

**Rule:** Produce where **AC = AR** (break-even, earning only **normal
profit**).

*(Adapt Diagram 1 for Sales Max)*\
On the same diagram, find the quantity where the **AC curve intersects
the AR curve**. Label this **Qsm**. Show that **Qsm \> Qrm \> Qpm** and
that the price **Psm** is the lowest of the three.

4\. Satisficing

**Objective:** Aiming for a **"satisfactory"** level of profit rather
than the maximum.

**Why?** Often due to the **principal-agent problem**. Managers (agents)
pursue their own goals (e.g., easier life, higher sales for prestige)
that **conflict** with shareholder (principal) goals.

**Result:** Output and price will lie **somewhere between** the profit
max and sales max points.

5\. Summary of Objectives

|  |  |  |  |  |
|----|----|----|----|----|
| Objective | Rule | Price | Output | Reason |
| Profit Maximisation | MC = MR | Highest | Lowest | Shareholder returns. |
| Revenue Maximisation | MR = 0 | Medium | Medium | Market share, manager incentives. |
| Sales Maximisation | AC = AR | Lowest | Highest | Market dominance, stock clearance. |

6\. Exam Focus: Key Takeaways

**Draw a single diagram** showing AR, MR, AC, and MC curves,
and **identify the output level** for each objective.

**Explain** the rationale (why) behind each different objective.

**Understand** the **principal-agent problem** as a key reason why firms
may not profit maximise.

**Compare** the likely outcomes (price, output, profit) for each
objective.
