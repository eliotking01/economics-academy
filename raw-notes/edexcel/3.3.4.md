Theme 3.3.4: Normal Profits, Supernormal Profits & Losses

1\. Key Definitions

**Explicit Costs:** **Actual money payments** for resources (e.g.,
wages, rent, materials).

**Implicit Costs:** The **opportunity cost** of using the entrepreneur's
own resources (e.g., forgone salary, forgone interest on capital).

Economic Cost: Total Cost (TC) = Explicit Costs + Implicit Costs.

**Normal Profit:** The **minimum profit** required to keep an
entrepreneur in an industry. It occurs when **Total Revenue (TR) = Total
Cost (TC)**, covering all **explicit and implicit costs**. This is
the **break-even** level of profit.

Supernormal (Abnormal) Profit: Any profit above normal profit (TR \>
TC).

Loss: When TR \< TC.

2\. Profit Maximisation Rule

- Firms maximise profit by producing where **Marginal Cost (MC) =
  Marginal Revenue (MR)**.

3\. Diagrammatic Representation of Profits & Losses

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 1: Supernormal Profit)\*

At Qpm, find the **Price (P)** from the AR curve and the **Average Cost
(C)** from the AC curve.

If P \> C, the firm earns supernormal profit. Shade the area: (P - C) ×

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 2: Loss-Making but Producing)\*

Use the same curves, but position the **AR curve below the AC

At **Qpm** (MC=MR), **P \< AC**, so the firm makes a **loss**. Shade the
area: **(C - P) × Qpm**.

4\. The Shut-Down Rules

When should a loss-making firm stop production?

a\) Short-Run Shut-Down Rule

- In the short run, **fixed costs must be paid** even if output is zero.

- Decision: Compare Price (AR) to Average Variable Cost (AVC).

  - **If AR \> AVC:** **Keep producing.** The revenue covers all
    variable costs and makes a **contribution towards fixed costs**.
    Loss is **less than total fixed costs**.

  - **If AR = AVC:** **Shut-down point.** Revenue only covers variable
    costs. Loss = total fixed costs.

  - **If AR \< AVC:** **Shut down immediately.** Revenue doesn't even
    cover variable costs. Loss is **greater than total fixed costs**.

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 3: Short-Run Shut-Down Point)\*\
Show the **AVC curve**. Mark the price where **AR (P) = Minimum point of
AVC**. This is the shut-down price.

b\) Long-Run Shut-Down Rule

- In the long run, **all costs are variable** (the firm can leave the
  industry).

- **Decision:** Compare Price (AR) to **Average Cost (AC)**.

  - **If AR \< AC:** The firm is making an **economic loss** (not
    covering all costs, including implicit). It should **leave the
    industry** in the long run.

loss.
