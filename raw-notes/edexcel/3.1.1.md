Theme 3.1.1: Sizes and Types of Firms

1\. Reasons Why Firms Grow

**Profit Motive:** To achieve higher profits and returns for
owners/shareholders.

**Cost Reduction:** To benefit from **economies of scale**, lowering
long-run average costs.

**Increased Market Power:** To gain monopoly power, influence prices,
and reduce competition.

**Diversification:** To spread risk across different markets and
products.

**Managerial Motives:** Growth can satisfy managerial ambitions and lead
to higher salaries/prestige.

2\. Reasons Why Firms Remain Small

**Market Niche:** Serving a specialised market with limited size but
high value.

**Personalised Service:** Offering bespoke products/services that larger
firms cannot.

**Ownership Goals:** Owners may prioritise lifestyle (**satisficing**)
over maximum growth/profit.

**Avoiding Diseconomies:** To prevent the inefficiencies and high costs
of large-scale operation.

**Market Structure:** Many operate in markets with **low barriers to
entry** and intense competition.

3\. Divorce of Ownership & Control (The Principal-Agent Problem)

In large firms (especially PLCs), owners (shareholders = **principals**)
hire managers (**agents**) to run the business.

**The Problem:** Managers may pursue their own goals (e.g., higher
salaries, sales maximisation, perks) that conflict with the owners' goal
of **profit maximisation**.

**Causes:** **Information gaps** (asymmetric information) allow managers
to hide their actions.

**Possible Solutions:** Performance-related pay, share options, closer
monitoring.

4\. Public vs. Private Sector Organisations

**Public Sector:** Owned and funded by the government. Primary goal is
to **provide a service** (e.g., NHS, state schools, BBC). Funded by
taxation.

**Private Sector:** Owned by private individuals or shareholders.
Primary goal is usually **profit**. Includes sole traders, partnerships,
and companies.

5\. Profit vs. Not-for-Profit Organisations

**Profit Organisations:** Aim to generate financial returns for owners.
Most private sector firms.

**Not-for-Profit (NFP) Organisations:** Aim to achieve a social,
environmental, or charitable mission. Any surplus is reinvested into the
cause. They operate in both private and public sectors (e.g., charities,
some social enterprises). They often receive tax advantages.

Key Exam Tips:

**Link to Market Structures:** Firm size is closely linked to
the **market structure** they operate in (e.g., monopolies tend to be
large; perfect competition features many small firms).

**Evaluation:** There is no "optimal" size. The choice depends on the
firm's objectives, market conditions, and ability to manage scale.

**Application:** Use real-world examples to illustrate concepts (e.g., a
local bakery vs. Tesco for size; the BBC vs. Netflix for sector).

**Key Terminology:** Ensure you can define and
apply **economies/diseconomies of scale**, **satisficing**, and
the **principal-agent problem**.
