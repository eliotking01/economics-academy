Theme 1.4.2: Government Failure

1\. Definition of Government Failure

Government failure occurs when **government intervention to correct a
market failure results in a net welfare loss** or a less efficient
allocation of resources than before the intervention. The cost of the
intervention outweighs the benefit.

2\. Causes of Government Failure

A. Distortion of Price Signals

Government price controls (e.g., minimum/maximum prices) disrupt the
market's signaling and incentive functions.

**Example:** A **minimum price** (e.g., for agricultural goods) signals
producers to increase supply, potentially leading to overproduction and
waste.

B. Unintended Consequences

Agents seek to maximise their own welfare, leading them to circumvent
policies.

**Examples:** Creating **black markets** (e.g., for illegal drugs),
cross-border shopping to avoid taxes, or finding loopholes that
undermine the policy's original goal.

C. Excessive Administrative Costs

- The cost of implementing, monitoring, and enforcing a policy (e.g.,
  regulation) can be greater than the social welfare gain it creates,
  representing poor value for money.

D. Information Gaps

- Governments suffer from **imperfect information** and **bounded
  rationality**, just like consumers and firms.

- This can lead to poorly designed policies that do not achieve their
  aims or have unforeseen negative effects.

E. Political Self-Interest & Short-Termism

- Policies may be designed to win votes rather than maximise social
  welfare, or focus on short-term political cycles rather than long-term
  economic benefits.

3\. Examples of Government Failure

**Regulatory Failure:** e.g., Environmental agencies failing to prevent
significant pollution; energy price caps failing to prevent excessive
profits while consumers struggle.

**Poorly Designed Policies:** e.g., Large public IT projects running
massively over budget and failing to meet objectives (e.g., NHS IT
programme).

**Unintended Consequences:** e.g., Indoor smoking bans leading to
increased outdoor heating and litter; alcohol minimum pricing leading to
cross-border shopping.

Key Exam Tips:

Core Concept: Government failure is the worsening of an outcome due to
intervention. It is not simply the *existence* of a market failure.

**Evaluation:** A key skill is weighing **potential government failure
against market failure**. You must argue whether intervention
is *likely* to make things better or worse in a given context.

**Application:** Use specific, real-world examples to illustrate the
different causes. Avoid vague statements.

**Balanced Argument:** Recognise that the possibility of government
failure is a strong argument for using **market-based solutions** (e.g.,
taxes, subsidies) over direct provision or heavy-handed regulation,
where possible.
