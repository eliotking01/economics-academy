Theme 3.4.2: Perfect Competition

1\. Key Characteristics

Many buyers and sellers.

Homogeneous (identical) products.

Perfect information available to all.

No barriers to entry or exit.

**Firms are price takers** – they accept the market price.

2\. The Firm's Demand Curve

Because firms are price takers, the demand curve they face
is **perfectly elastic** (horizontal).

- Price (P) = Marginal Revenue (MR) = Average Revenue (AR).

- The market price is set by **industry supply and demand**.

4\. Long-Run Equilibrium: Normal Profit Only

The **freedom of entry and exit** drives the market to long-run
equilibrium where all firms make **normal profit** (AR = AC).

Process from Short-Run Profit to Long-Run Equilibrium:

**Short-Run:** Firms make supernormal profit (Diagram 2).

Entry: Profit attracts new firms. Industry Supply increases (shifts
right).

**Price Falls:** Market price falls (e.g., P1 → P2).

**New Equilibrium:** At the new lower price, the firm's horizontal
demand curve falls until it is **tangent to the lowest point of the AC
curve**. Here, **P = AR = AC = MC**. Supernormal profit is eliminated;
firms earn **normal profit**.

Process from Short-Run Loss to Long-Run Equilibrium:

**Short-Run:** Firms make losses (Diagram 3).

Exit: Losses cause some firms to leave. Industry Supply decreases
(shifts left).

**Price Rises:** Market price rises.

**New Equilibrium:** Price rises until the firm's demand curve is
tangent to the lowest point of AC. Firms earn **normal profit**.

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 4: Long-Run Equilibrium)\*\
Draw the firm with **MC** and **AC**. Draw the horizontal **P = AR =
MR** line **tangent to the minimum point of the AC curve**. The firm
produces where **P = MC = min AC**.

5\. Efficiency in Long-Run Equilibrium

Productive Efficiency: Yes – producing at minimum AC.

Allocative Efficiency: Yes – because P = MC.

**Dynamic Efficiency:** **Unlikely** – no supernormal profit for R&D
investment.

shifts**.

**Identify** the **long-run equilibrium point** on a firm's diagram (P =
MC = min AC).

**Analyse** the **efficiency** of perfect competition in the long run.
