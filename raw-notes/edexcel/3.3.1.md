Theme 3.3.1: Revenue

1\. Key Definitions

Total Revenue (TR): Price (P) × Quantity (Q)

Average Revenue (AR): TR / Q (This is the price per unit and the
firm's demand curve).

Marginal Revenue (MR): ΔTR / ΔQ (The additional revenue from selling one
more unit).

2\. Revenue in Perfect Competition (Price Taker)

Firms are **price takers**; they sell at the **market price**.

**AR = MR = Price.** The demand curve is **perfectly
elastic** (horizontal).

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 1: Perfect Competition Revenue)\*

Draw axes: Cost/Revenue (£) vs Quantity (Q).

Draw a horizontal line labelled D = AR = MR = P.

The **TR curve** would be an **upward-sloping straight line** from the
origin (as TR rises at a constant rate).

3\. Revenue in Imperfect Competition (Price Maker)

Firms have **market power**; to sell more, they must **lower the
price**.

AR (Demand) is downward sloping. MR is also downward sloping and lies
below AR.

\*(Hand-draw Diagram 2: Imperfect Competition Revenue)\*

Draw axes: Cost/Revenue (£) vs Quantity (Q).

Draw a **downward-sloping** straight line labelled **D = AR**.

Draw a **steeper** downward-sloping line below it, starting at the same
point on the Y-axis. Label this **MR**. (Key: The **MR curve is twice as
steep as the AR curve**).

Mark where **MR = 0**. At this point, **TR is maximised**.

4\. The Total Revenue Rule & Price Elasticity of Demand (PED)

The relationship between **PED** and **TR** is crucial for pricing
decisions.

|  |  |  |
|----|----|----|
| If Demand is... | And the firm... | Then Total Revenue will... |
| Price ELASTIC (PED \> 1) | Lowers Price | Increase |
| Price ELASTIC (PED \> 1) | Raises Price | Decrease |
| Price INELASTIC (PED \< 1) | Raises Price | Increase |
| Price INELASTIC (PED \< 1) | Lowers Price | Decrease |
| Unitary Elastic (PED = 1) | Changes Price | **Remain unchanged** (MR = 0, TR is maximised). |

5\. Summary of Key Relationships

AR is always the demand curve.

MR is related to PED: When MR is positive, demand is elastic. When MR
is negative, demand is inelastic. TR is maximised where MR = 0 (PED =
1).

**TR Curve Shape:** In imperfect competition, TR rises at a decreasing
rate until MR=0, then falls.

6\. Exam Focus: Key Takeaways

AR/MR** curve of a price taker and the **downward-sloping AR &
MR** curves of a price maker.

**Explain** why the MR curve is below the AR curve in imperfect markets.

**Apply the Total Revenue Rule:** Analyse how a price change will affect
TR given the PED of the product.
