2.1.1 Economic Growth

Edexcel A-Level Economics (9EC0) | Theme 2.1.1

Specification Coverage: Students must understand how GDP is calculated (income/expenditure approaches), distinguish between nominal/real GDP, and evaluate limitations of GDP as a measure of living standards. Analysis should include PPP adjustments and alternative measures like GNI/GNP.

Measuring Economic Growth

Gross Domestic Product (GDP): The total market value of all final goods/services produced within a country's borders in a given period.

Expenditure Approach

GDP = C + I + G + (X-M)

  • C: Household consumption (£1.5tn UK 2023)
  • I: Business investment (£369bn)
  • G: Government spending (£892bn)
  • X-M: Net exports (-£78bn deficit)

Income Approach

GDP = W + R + I + P

  • W: Wages (56% of UK GDP)
  • R: Rent (8%)
  • I: Interest (5%)
  • P: Profit (31%)
Calculation Protocol: In exams, nominal GDP must be converted to real GDP using: \[ \text{Real GDP} = \frac{\text{Nominal GDP}}{\text{GDP Deflator}} \times 100 \]

Real vs Nominal GDP

Type Definition UK Example (2023) Usefulness
Nominal GDP Current prices, not inflation-adjusted £2.45 trillion Shows monetary size of economy
Real GDP Constant prices, inflation-adjusted £2.21 trillion (2019 prices) Measures actual output growth
GDP per capita Real GDP ÷ population £32,876 Living standards comparison

Conversion Example

Given: 2023 Nominal GDP = £2.45tn | GDP Deflator = 110.7 (2019=100)
Real GDP: £2.45tn ÷ 1.107 = £2.21tn at 2019 prices

Beyond GDP: Alternative Measures

Measure Calculation UK (2023) Advantage
GNI GDP + net income from abroad £2.52tn (+£70bn) Includes multinational profits
GNP GNI - income sent abroad £2.48tn (-£40bn) Shows actual national income
PPP Adjusted GDP adjusted for price differences $3.12tn (IMF) Better living standard comparison
Developing vs Developed: Nigeria's GDP is $477bn but GNI only $441bn (-7.5%) due to oil multinationals. UK difference is +2.9% (ONS).

Limitations of GDP

Key Criticisms

1. Inequality Blindness
  • UK GDP/capita = £32,876 but median wage = £27,756
  • Top 10% own 43% of wealth (IFS 2023)
2. Non-Market Activities
  • Unpaid childcare worth £140bn/year excluded
  • Black economy (£150bn) not captured
3. Quality Changes
  • Smartphones now 10× more powerful but priced similarly
  • NHS waiting lists mask healthcare "output"
4. Environmental Costs
  • UK natural capital declined 30% since 1990
  • Climate damage = 3% GDP by 2050 (BoE)

Alternative Wellbeing Measures

Measure Components UK Ranking Insight
Human Development Index Life expectancy, education, GNI 18th (UNDP 2023) US ranks lower due to inequality
ONS Wellbeing Health, relationships, environment Score 7.5/10 Stagnant since 2015 despite GDP growth
Easterlin Paradox Happiness vs income Plateau at £35k Explains US-UK happiness gap
Evaluation Framework: When comparing countries, always consider:
  1. Real vs nominal measures
  2. PPP adjustments
  3. Non-GDP factors (inequality, hours worked)
  4. Data reliability (China's 5% growth vs 2% electricity use)
