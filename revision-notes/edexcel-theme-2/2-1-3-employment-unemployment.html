2.1.3 Employment & Unemployment

Edexcel A-Level Economics (9EC0) | Theme 2.1.3

Specification Coverage: Students must understand how unemployment is measured (ILO vs Claimant Count), analyse different types of unemployment (structural, cyclical etc.), and evaluate the impact of migration on labour markets. Analysis should include current UK data and policy responses.

Key Definitions

Term Definition UK Data (Q1 2024)
Unemployed Actively seeking work + available to start 1.4 million (ILO)
Economically Inactive Not seeking work (students, carers etc.) 9.2 million (22.2%)
Underemployed Want more hours/better matched job 1.1 million (ONS)
Employment Rate % of working-age population in work 75.9% (16-64 year olds)
Key Formula: \[ \text{Unemployment Rate} = \frac{\text{Unemployed}}{\text{Labour Force}} \times 100 \] UK Labour Force = 34.5m (employed + unemployed)

Claimant Count Jobseeker's Allowance claimants • Real-time data

2024 Discrepancy: ILO rate = 4.2% (1.4m) vs Claimant Count = 3.9% (1.1m) due to:
  • Universal Credit changes excluding 180,000
  • Self-employed not claiming benefits

Types of Unemployment

1. Structural

  • Cause: Industry decline (e.g., UK coal mining)
  • 2024 Example: Retail jobs ↓15% since 2019
  • Solution: Retraining (£2.5bn Skills Fund)

2. Cyclical

  • Cause: Recession (AD↓)
  • 2024 Example: Tech sector layoffs (5,000+)
  • Solution: Fiscal/monetary stimulus

3. Frictional

  • Cause: Job search time
  • 2024 Example: Graduates (3-month average)
  • Solution: Improved job matching

4. Real Wage

  • Cause: Minimum wage above equilibrium
  • 2024 Example: Youth unemployment = 11.6%
  • Solution: Productivity improvements

Key Studies

  1. Bank of England (2023): 10% migrant ↑ → 2% wage ↓ for poorest decile
  2. Migration Advisory Committee: No evidence migrants "take jobs" - fill skills gaps
  3. ONS: Migrants 15% more likely to be self-employed
